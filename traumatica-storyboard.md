# TRAUMATICA: 5 STAGES — STORYBOARD DRAFT

---

## OPENING SEQUENCE

**Black screen. The sound of a washing machine humming.**

Then — a sharp crack. White flash. Silence.

Text fades in, one line at a time:

> *"It wasn't a stage. It wasn't a studio. It wasn't a show."*
> *"It was a laundry machine."*
> *"And everything stopped."*

A brief chaotic sequence — the hospital. Bright lights. Beeping. Movement.
DOMINO (bandaged) is in a bed. Nurses pass. The room spins slightly.

Then — it goes quiet.

Text:
> *"Weeks in bed. Months to recover."*
> *"This is what happened inside."*

---

## THE WORLD

The entire game takes place inside **THE BED** — a vast, surreal dreamscape that expands from the mattress outward. The room is dark at the edges. Familiar but distorted. Explorable in any order.

The player is free to wander. The five stages don't have to be faced in sequence — because the experience wasn't linear. The mind doesn't grieve in order.

---

## THE NPCS

---

### NURSE BETTY — *Denial*

She's kind. Clinical. She believes everything she says.
But her comfort is a cage.

**Dialogue:**

> "You're stable. That's what matters."
> "The brain just needs rest. Weeks. Maybe more."
> "Try not to think too hard. You'll strain it."
> "No screens. No music. No... output. Just be still."

*DOMINO internal:*
> *"I have sessions booked. I have a show. I can't just—"*
> *"I can't just stop."*

**What she holds:**
She holds the illusion that this is temporary and manageable — that if you just follow the rules, you'll be back to yourself by next week.

**What you have to do:**
Sit still long enough to realize — she's not wrong. She's just not the whole truth.

---

### THE RAGE — *Anger*

He's loud. Hot. He never stops moving.
He says what you were too exhausted to scream.

**Dialogue:**

> "A LAUNDRY MACHINE. You almost lost everything to a LAUNDRY MACHINE."
> "You were building something. You had momentum. And it just — gone."
> "While you're stuck in this bed, everyone else is moving."
> "Every day you're here is a day someone else takes your spot."
> "You were finally being SEEN. And now — silence."
> "That's what hurts the most, isn't it. The silence."

*DOMINO internal:*
> *"Yes."*

**What you have to do:**
Let him say all of it. Don't run. Don't fix it.
When he's done — he collapses. Exhausted. And for a second you see he was never angry at you.
He was just scared.

**After The Rage encounter — THE SNAKE NIGHTMARE begins.**

---

### THE SNAKE NIGHTMARE — *Rage → Hatred*

The screen distorts. The bed-world glitches.

Text:
> *"Rage unprocessed doesn't disappear."*
> *"It evolves."*

DOMINO is pulled into a nightmare version of the world.

**The environment:**
Laundry machines — everywhere. They fill the space. Some shake and rattle. Some are silent and still. They appear at random, multiplying the longer you're in the nightmare. The sound of the original impact plays faintly on loop, getting louder the longer the game goes on.

**The snake:**
The snake IS the rage. It starts small — manageable, something you recognize.
But every time it feeds, it grows. Darker. Faster.
At a certain length, the color shifts — burnt orange bleeding into deep red bleeding into black.
That's the hatred. Rage that found something to eat and kept eating.

**The mechanic:**
- Laundry machines are obstacles scattered across the board
- They multiply over time — the trauma doesn't stay still
- The snake grows with each piece consumed (as normal)
- *But* — the goal isn't survival forever. The goal is to navigate through it for long enough to reach the exit: a small light that appears at the far edge of the board
- If the snake becomes hatred (grows past a threshold length) — nightmare intensifies, machines shake, screen flickers
- You don't have to destroy the snake. You have to survive it long enough to find the way out.

**On exit (win condition):**
Screen goes white. The snake sound fades. The laundry machine sound fades.
Silence.

Text:
> *"You didn't kill it."*
> *"You moved through it."*
> *"That's enough."*

DOMINO returns to the bed-world. The Rage NPC is gone. The room is quieter.

### THE KEEPER — *Bargaining*

Old. Still. Holds something wrapped in cloth — you can't see what it is yet.
He speaks slowly. He's been waiting.

**Dialogue:**

> "You want to go back."
> "Back to the version of you who was always running."
> "I understand. He worked hard. He built real things."
> *pause*
> "But he was also running *from* something."
> "You know this."
> "God didn't take him from you as punishment."
> "He was removed so something new could breathe."

*DOMINO:*
> "I'm not ready to let him go."

> "I know. But you already know what I'm holding."

*He unwraps the cloth. It's empty.*

> "The old you isn't in here. He never was."
> "He was a version you needed then."
> "What you need now... requires more room."
> "Let him go. Not because he wasn't real."
> "Because what's coming is."

---

### THE SHADOW — *Depression*

He whispers. He never looks at you directly.
He asks questions instead of making statements.

**Dialogue:**

> *"Do you remember your first show?"*

*[player has to answer — yes / barely / no]*

> *"Do you remember her name? The one who believed in you first?"*

> *"It's slipping, isn't it."*
> *"Not all at once. Just... piece by piece."*
> *"What if you never fully come back?"*
> *"What if this is just... what's left?"*
> *"A half version. Present enough to function. Not enough to create."*
> *"What if the music is gone and you just don't know it yet?"*

*DOMINO internal:*
> *"Stop."*

> *"That's the fear talking."*
> *"The PTSD. The brain that can't trust itself anymore."*
> *"But what if I'm right?"*

**What you have to do:**
Look at him. Directly. For a long time.
He gets smaller the longer you look.
He was never as big as he sounded in the dark.

---

### THE GUIDE — *Acceptance*

Warm. Unhurried. She doesn't look like anyone you know,
but feels like everyone who ever told you the truth gently.

**Dialogue:**

> "You made it through all of it."
> "Not past it. Through it."
> *pause*
> "The accident didn't happen to you."
> "It happened *for* you."
> "I know that's hard to hold. You don't have to hold it all at once."
> "But nothing was taken. Only the parts that were never yours to keep."
> "The sprint? That was fear with a good work ethic."
> "What's left is real."
> *pause*
> "The pause is not the enemy."
> "The pause is the practice."
> "You get to bring it with you."

---

### FAMILIAR FACE — *Inner Child*
*(can be found at any point — he's always been here)*

A young version of DOMINO. Bandages on. Sitting quietly in a corner of the bed-world.
He's not sad. He's just been waiting.

**Dialogue — when you find him:**

> *"I knew you'd come."*
> *"I've been here the whole time."*
> *"I kept the lights on."*

*DOMINO:*
> *"I'm sorry it took so long."*

> *"It took exactly as long as it needed to."*
> *"But I need you to know something."*
> *"We don't have to rush anymore."*
> *"The rushing was never about the music."*
> *"It was about feeling like we weren't enough if we stopped."*
> *"We were always enough."*
> *"We just needed someone to tell us."*

*DOMINO:*
> *"I've got you."*

> *"I know."*
> *"I've always known you would."*
> *"I just had to wait for you to know it too."*

## THE ENDING

After all five stages — after Familiar Face —

DOMINO stands in the center of the bed.

The room gets quieter. Brighter at the edges.

Text appears. Slow. One line at a time.

> *"The accident didn't end the story."*
> *"It paused it."*
> *" "*
> *"And in the pause —"*
> *"you found what you'd been running from."*
> *" "*
> *"Yourself."*
> *" "*
> *"The long pause doesn't end here."*
> *"It begins here."*

**Fade to black.**

Then — a single line:

> *"— THE LONG PAUSE"*

*(link / transition to the book)*

## NOTES ON TONE

- Exaggerated and metaphorical — but the emotional truth is real and unfiltered
- No melodrama. The heaviest lines land through stillness, not volume
- DOMINO's internal voice is always honest, never performative
- The NPCs are not villains — they are all parts of him
- The inner child is the emotional center of the whole game
